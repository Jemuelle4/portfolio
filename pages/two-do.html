TWO DO

24 hours

4 people

Two Do is a project made during the Protothon hosted by UW DubsTech. Grouped with three other people, we designed an app that will help long distanced loved ones come together in this digital world.

Research

Research Methods

  • 31 survey participants
  • 2 one-on-one interviews
  • Market research and competetive analysis

Findings

  • Participants cited lack of physical touch followed by communication issues as most frustrating aspects of long-distance relationships
  • Feelings of excitement, anticipation, and fun are highly important in a relationship
  • Common communication methods include messaging and video apps, but they often did not meet these needs or it gets repetitive easily
  • Common activities and topics gets repetitive quickly and participants has trouble finding new activties to do.

Despite having physical touch as the most frustrating aspects in a long-distanced relationship, we knew that it would be difficult to design a product in one day that utilizes wearable technology. Our group are inexperienced in the field of wearable technology as well.

User Persona

Our group brainstormed the characteristics for our user based on the research findings. At the very end, we all discussed and combined our ideas to make a simple, yet realistic user persona.

Meet Fang

  • Full-time college student
  • Work part time as a front desk agent
  • Likes watching Netflix movies, cooking new recipes, and hanging out with Oslo, a three-legged husky

Having been in a long distance relationship for over a year, Fang feels like there's nothing interesting to do with their partner anymore.

Pain Points

  1. Lack of interesting activities to engage in with their partner
  2. Difficult to schedule one-on-one time due to busy schedule
  3. Not up to date with partner's current interests due to distance

As a result

Fang feelds disconnected from their partner

So... how might we help Fang discover interesting activities to experience with their partner so that they can feel more connected?

Ideation

Once our group knew our users and the pain points that they have, we started brainstorming about features that we can incorporate into our app.

There were many discussions as to how we should approach the app. Our initial discussion focused a lot on topic generators and love languages. However, we stepped back and read our "How Might We" statement again and realized that our initial idea did not properly address the pain points.

Then we started talking about the topic of activities. During the discussion, it was also pointed out that a prompt generator is boring and overdone, but if we were to suggest activities, it would allow for better qualitiy, fun time. People doing activities together generates topics to talk about as well, as both person would have a shared experience to relate back to.

With a clear focus, we started ideating using a prioritization matrix.

Since we only had a day to design, we prioritized adding more high value, low effort features into our app.

Design

Once we had an idea of our features, we started designing a task flow.

Task Flow

One important feature of this app is to ensure a way to interact without needing to sign up. We extensively discussed how users in general find it frustrating to sign up to a lot of applications without knowing how the app works first. Therefore, we wanted to provide a way so that the users can use the app without an account; asking them to sign up only after they are confident with the app and unlock additional features.
